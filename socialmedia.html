Social Media

From the start of 19th century, there are several steps by humans to develop and deploy better ways of communication between each other. The revolution started with the discovery of radio waves. New gadgets were invented that totally changed how humans used to view technology. Television, Radio and Telephones started a boom in communication technology. Trade, warfare and media were the major fields experiencing this revolution.

After the second world war, it had become obvious that the nation having advanced technologies will dominate over others. The race for communication devices started again. During the cold war, fight increased and it led to the invention of “Internet”, one of the most powerful tools humans created. In the year 1997, first social media site “Six Degrees” came into existence. Since then, hustle started to develop new and better social media platforms that will decrease the gap in communication and ease the connection between world communities. Emails, Facebook,Twitter, Instagram, Snapchat, WhatsApp, LinkedIn are all examples of how world wide web changed people’s lives.

Google

Founded by Larry Page and Sergey Brin in the year 1998, Google is world’s number one search engine in terms of users. The parent organisation Alphabet owns various sub companies namely Google Chrome, YouTube, Gmail and others. Being one of the largest tech giants on the planet, google provides services in a variety of sectors like google maps, google cloud service, google drive, google meet and many more.

Twitter

Twitter is an American microblogging and social networking service on which users post and interact with messages known as "tweets". Registered users can post, like, and retweet tweets, but unregistered users can only read them. Users access Twitter through its website interface, through Short Message Service (SMS) or its mobile-device application software ("app").Twitter was created by Jack Dorsey, Noah Glass, Biz Stone, and Evan Williams in March 2006
